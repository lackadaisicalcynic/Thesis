[Evidence-based choice and antidepressants treatment in psychiatry].  Depression is a severe and usually recurrent mental disorder which leads to a prominent impairment of physical and social functioning, a reduced quality of life of the patients, implies as well an increased morbidity and mortality.
Reducing the social-economic depression burden is possible in the optimization of decision-making process of antidepressant choice (including SSRIs and SNRIs) based on the evidence-based risk/benefit ratio and resource saving potential treatment involving target psychosocial interventions and monitoring of long-term treatment adherence.
