[Multi-session stereotactic radiotherapy in the treatment of recurrent B-cell primary intraocular lymphoma (a case report)].
The article describes the clinical observation of a 41-year-old woman suffering from non-Hodgkin's B-cell primary lymphoma of the central nervous system involving both eyes.
The article features the data on 85-month follow-up, as well as description of the clinical and instrumental features of the disease.  The study provides analysis of the long-term results of combined treatment of recurrent primary intraocular lymphoma including repeated courses of radiation therapy.
