[Clinical manifestations of the onset and diagnostic problems in children with moyamoya disease].
Moyamoya disease in children is a rarely diagnosed entity.  Frequent headache is the first symptom of the disease.
Headache associated with moya-moya disease (HAMD) is a separated entity reported in the literature.
Variants of onset, a spectrum of primary diagnoses and diagnosis verification rate of moyamoya disease in 7 children are presented.
A clinical case of moya-moya disease in a 9-year boy, who was primarily diagnosed with migraine with aura, is reported.
The results presented by the authors as well as literature data indicate the necessity of using magnetic-resonance angiography in children with headache attacks and focal neurologic symptoms to identify this progressive vascular pathology and timely recommend a surgical treatment.
