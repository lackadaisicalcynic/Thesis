[The application of the modern radiodiagnostic methods in otorhinolaryngology].  This paper highlights the problems and difficulties associated with the introduction of the modern radiodiagnostic methods including computed and magnetic resonance tomography into the otorhinolaryngological practice in this country.
The approaches to addressing these problems and overcoming difficulties are proposed.  The review of the diagnostic potential of various modalities is presented.
The modern algorithms for the examination of the patients presenting with various diseases of the ears, nose, and throat are described with special reference to their diagnostic value.
