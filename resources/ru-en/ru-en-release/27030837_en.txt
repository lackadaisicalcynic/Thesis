[Trigeminal autonomic cephalgias].  Review of literature on the trigeminal autonomic cephalgias are presented.
Trigeminal autonomic cephalgias are primary headaches with phenotype consisting of trigeminal pain with autonomic sign including lacrimation, rhinorrhea and miosis.
Discussed are issues of classification, pathogenesis, clinical picture, diagnosis, differential diagnosis and treatment of this headache.
Special attention is paid to cluster headache, paroxysmal hemicrania, SUNCT syndrome, hemicrania continua.
