[The differential diagnosis of amyotrophic lateral sclerosis and subacute herpes virus myelitis].  Differential diagnosis of incurable and potentially curable neurological diseases is an urgent problem of modern neurology.
The authors present a case report of subacute herpes virus myelitis, a rare complication of herpes infection by Varicella-Zoster virus.  The differential diagnosis with amyotrophic lateral sclerosis is described.
