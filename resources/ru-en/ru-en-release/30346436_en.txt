Essential tremor (ET) is the most common extrapyramidal disease and one of the most frequent neurological diseases.  The main presentation of ET is a progressive bilateral (kinetic-postural) hand tremor.
The prevalence in people over 65 years is 5%, it increases with age up to 22% in people over 95 years.  About half of patients with ET have family history.
Possible methods of medical and surgical management are described.
